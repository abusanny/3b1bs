# Visual proof for \( (a + b)^3 \)

This short animation builds a cube whose side length is `a + b`. To show why its
volume equals the famous expression `(a + b)^3`, the cube is gently divided
along the three coordinate directions so that everyone can follow the pieces.

Each section of the cube is colour coded and labelled:

1. **`a^3`** – a smaller cube of side `a`.
2. **`3a^2 b`** – three prisms which look like thick square sheets.
3. **`3a b^2`** – three prisms that resemble long bars.
4. **`b^3`** – a tiny cube of side `b` in the far corner.

When we add up the volumes of these eight pieces, we have

\[ (a + b)^3 = a^3 + 3a^2 b + 3a b^2 + b^3. \]

The explanation is visual rather than technical, with clear labels and smooth
camera moves.  Even young viewers can see that the whole cube is nothing more
than the collection of its differently coloured parts.

For extra clarity the outer edges of the cube are also highlighted:

* **Red** edges mark the cube's length.
* **Green** edges mark the width.
* **Blue** edges mark the height.

Along each edge a small arrow shows how the segment splits into a piece of
length ``a`` followed by a piece of length ``b``.  These coloured markers help
any audience, young or old, keep track of where every block fits.
